1 About Instructions for Use
This guide provides detailed instructions for the effective use of the DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite. It
provides guidance for all users and should be reviewed before logging into the application.
Before accessing the Diagnostic Suite, read the Instructions for Use (IFU) carefully, especially the
Notices and WARNINGS sections, as they contain critical safety and operational information.
Additionally, pay close attention to on-screen messages and online help, which also include important
Notices and WARNINGS related to device functionality.
NOTICE
Notices are designed to highlight points of attention to aid users.
Step 1: Take note of any notices that appear in the software solution.
Step 2: Use these notices as guides to enhance your user experience and efficiency.
WARNING
WARNINGS are instructions that, if ignored, may lead to misinterpretation and/or loss or damage
of patient-related data.
Step 1: Pay close attention to any warnings that appear in the software solution.
Step 2: Follow the instructions provided in the warning to avoid any potential harm or data loss.
Terminology
Term Description
DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite The DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite is a comprehensive suite of AI-
powered applications designed to enhance radiology workflows. Built on
the robust DeepHealth OS platform, the suite offers a range of tools
tailored to specific needs within radiology.
Facility Group A Facility Group involves creating or configuring groups that represent
various entities within a healthcare system, such as hospitals, clinics,
laboratories, or administrative offices.
Facility A Facility represents a specific site—such as a hospital, clinic, or
laboratory—with unique operational, access, and security requirements.
Radiologist DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite, radiologists play a critical role in
analyzing medical images, interpreting findings, and ensuring accurate
diagnoses using an AI-enhanced workflow.

4 DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite
Device Description
The Diagnostic Suite is a comprehensive, cloud-native platform designed to support medical imaging
operations and workflow management. It provides a unified and scalable workspace that includes
image/data management, worklist management, reporting workflow support, and integration with
external components such as Core PACS, diagnostic viewers and AI Orchestrator (AIO). Diagnostic
viewing and AI inference are performed are performed by separate integrated products/devices;
Diagnostic Suite does not perform clinical image interpretation or generate diagnostic conclusions.
It also supports teleradiology, integrates effortlessly with existing healthcare systems, and incorporates
cutting-edge visualization tools. Together, these features address the growing demands of modern
healthcare, ensuring a seamless experience for radiologists and healthcare providers while prioritizing
both efficiency and scalability.
Indications For Use
The Diagnostic Suite is a flexible, modular software platform designed to support healthcare providers
in efficiently managing medical imaging studies. It suports the imaging workflow — from receipt of
studies and related metadata, through storage, workflow management, reporting support, and

12 Diagnostic Suite Radiologist
Overview
In the DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite, radiologists play a critical role in analyzing medical images,
interpreting findings, and ensuring accurate diagnoses using an AI-enhanced workflow. Each radiologist
is assigned a primary Facility Group (FG), which is mandatory and serves as their main working group.
This primary FG establishes their default context and optimizes their daily workflow.
Additionally, radiologists can be assigned to one or more secondary Facility Groups, offering the
flexibility to access and interpret exams across multiple facilities. This multi-FG assignment enables
them to efficiently manage their workload, collaborate with colleagues at different locations, and
contribute to broader healthcare coverage. The system ensures that, even with access to multiple FGs,
radiologists adhere to each facility's specific workflows and protocols, maintaining compliance and
operational consistency.
1. Select Radiologist to open the radiologist home page.
Notice: You must log in as a Diagnostic Suite administrator to create and manage radiologist
operations.
Radiologist’s Key Responsibilities
• Exam Review & Interpretation: Access and analyze DICOM images and HL7 patient data
through an integrated interface.
• Report Generation & Signing: Create, edit, and finalize structured diagnostic reports.
• Consultation & Collaboration: Request second opinions or specialist input through the Consult
Request feature.
• Workflow Management: Organize exams using the To Read, Drafted, Pending, and Signed tabs
for streamlined case handling.
• Audit & Compliance: Maintain compliance with healthcare regulations via the Audit Trail feature.

Patient registration and matching behavior
• Patient registration: Currently, new patients cannot be manually registered in the platform. Instead,
new patients are automatically added to the Patients Menu list when a new exam is created in the
system.
• Patient matching behavior: The patients matching functionality ensures that new patient data is
matched with existing records. The matching process follows these steps:
The following table describes the steps for a patient matching behavior:
Steps Description
Step 1 The system first searches for patients within the same facility and matches the MRN (Medical
Record Number) to the received data.
Step 2 If no match is found, the system will attempt to find a match within the same facility using the
patient's gender, date of birth (DOB), first name, and last name.
Step 3 If the patient’s name is missing in the DICOM (DCM) file, a new patient record with empty
first name and last name will be created automatically.

Study Location Registration
Overview
The Study Location Registration feature enables the system to track where a study originates, where
it is stored, and where it can be accessed from. This supports scenarios such as hybrid deployments,
data synchronization, and smart migration.
Each study can be associated with multiple devices (sources and locations), allowing the system to:
• Track study origin (one or more sources)
• Identify available locations for opening/streaming
• Monitor migration and processing (cooking) status
• Determine whether a study is available locally or in the cloud
In the user interface, this is reflected through the Study Location field and related indicators such as
migration status and tags.

• Access to the application (no specific UI configuration required)

RVU Management
Overview
The system introduces shift-based logic for duty assignment to ensure that Radiologists are assigned
orders only during their active shifts and within their configured Relative Value Unit (RVU) capacity.
Assignment decisions are now based on:
• Radiologist shift availability (individual or facility group level)
• Shift-specific workload (signed and in-flight/To Read orders)
• Configured queue depth (RVU capacity)
This enhancement ensures accurate workload distribution and prevents assignment to Radiologists who
are either out of shift or have reached their capacity.
Prerequisites
• Access to the application with appropriate configuration privileges
• Radiologists assigned to duties
• Radiologist shift availability configured (optional but recommended)
• Facility group shift configuration available (fallback mechanism)
• Queue depth (RVU capacity) configured for Radiologists
• Incoming orders with defined RVU values

• Data Encryption at Rest:
The DeepHealth Diagnostic Suiteapplication, hosted on Google Cloud, leverages Google's
o
default encryption at rest capabilities to protect customer data.
This means all customer content generated or provided to Google through DeepHealth
o
Diagnostic Suite (data stored in services like Cloud Storage, Persistent Disk, etc.) is
automatically encrypted at the storage layer without requiring any specific action from the
application developers or users.
• Multi-layered encryption approach:
Storage System Level: Data is broken into chunks, each encrypted with a unique Data
o
Encryption Key (DEK) using AES-256 (with a small exception of legacy Persistent Disks using
AES-128). If a chunk is updated, a new key is generated. Access is controlled via Access
Control Lists (ACLs), ensuring only authorized Google services can decrypt.
Storage Device Level: Further encryption is applied at the hardware level (HDDs and SSDs)
o
using AES-256 (or AES-128 for legacy HDDs) with separate device-level keys.
Backup Level: The backup system maintains encryption throughout the process, with most
o
backup files independently encrypted using their own DEKs derived from keys stored in
Keystore. Metadata is encrypted separately as well.
Key Management: Google manages the DEKs, encrypting them using Key Encryption Keys
o
(KEKs) stored in Keystore (a central key repository). The KEKs themselves are protected by
a hierarchy culminating in the root keystore master key distributor for high availability and
security. For Google Cloud customers, Cloud KMS provides additional control, enabling the
creation and management of custom encryption keys for envelope encryption. However,
DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite default at-rest encryption relies on Google's managed key
infrastructure.
Password Security: Use strong, unique passwords and avoid sharing credentials with others.
o
Regularly update passwords and avoid reusing old passwords.
Session Management: Log out after completing a session to prevent unauthorized access.
o
Avoid leaving active sessions unattended.
Avoid Public Networks: Connect only via secure networks or VPNs. Avoid using public Wi-
o
Fi for accessing DeepHealth Diagnostic Suite.
Role Validation: Assign user roles based on the principle of least privilege and review roles
o
periodically to ensure relevance.
